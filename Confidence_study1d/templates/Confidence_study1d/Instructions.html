Instructions
{% endblock %} {% block content %}

In this experiment you are asked to choose from pairs of money machines. Your take-home money depends on your choices. Each money machine's possible outcomes are clearly stated. They can be positive as well as negative. Sometimes they are certain, and at other times the probabilities of the outcomes are unknown. In those unknown cases, any probability is just as likely as any other.

To help you choose a money machine, we provide you with the record of previous plays at each machine: these are randomly drawn samples. When you are ready to make a binding choice, please click on your preferred money machine.
We will offer you a statistical tool to aid your decision making. There is an illustration on the next page.

In total, there are {{Constants.num_problemsets}} pairs of money machines.

After you have made all your choices, we will randomly select one pair of money machines and play out your choices for that pair. This means you should consider each decision as if it were your only one. The outcome from your chosen money machine will be added to a show-up fee of ${{Constants.showupfee}}, which together make up the total payment you receive at the end of the experiment. Therefore, the total payment you receive at the end can be bigger as well as smaller than ${{Constants.showupfee}}.

Good luck.
