In this experiment you are asked to choose between two points machines. Your take-home money depends on your choices. Each points machine’s possible outcomes are the points you can earn. They are clearly stated, as shown in the example below. They can be positive as well as negative. The probability for each outcome is clearly stated.

An example pair of points machines is shown below.

Points Machine A

Points Machine B

Whenever you are ready to make a choice, you can select your preferred points machine by clicking on it.

In total, there are {{Constants.num_problemsets}} pairs of points machines.

You should consider each decision as if it were your only one. This is because, after you have made all your choices, we will randomly select one pair of points machines and play out your choice for that pair. The outcome from your chosen points machine will be added to, or subtracted from, your base endowment of {{Constants.showupfee}} points, which together make up the total points you have at the end of the experiment.

Your total points determine your chances of winning the cash prize. Each point works like a lottery ticket with your name on it. In the end, we will draw one ticket from among all participants' tickets. The more points/tickets you have, the more likely you are to win.

Please click on the “Next” button to begin. Good luck.
